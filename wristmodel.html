Surgical Wrist Modeling

Primary Researcher
March 2022 - Present

Project Overview



70% Complete

Project Goal:

Optimize wrist design for maximum grip strength to guide surgical decisionmaking and improve intervention for patients with wrist osteoarthritis.

Methods:

  • Validated lab's novel wrist and hand model in various releases of OpenSim for a variety of simulation tasks
  • Augmented wrist and hand model to represent surgical salvage procedures using parameters from previous data collection studies
  • Used a global optimization algorithm to acquire the activation patterns that result in the greatest grip force
  • Conducted a sensitivity study to identify the effect of various biomechanical factors on grip force
  • Ran a torque analysis across wrist range of motion to improve the optimization cost function

Key Results:

  • Identified wrist posture as the greatest determinant of grip strength
  • Discovered destabilization of wrist posture and grip strength when altering any model parameter
  • Emphasized need for consistent wrist posture among models during simulation and patients during data collection
  • Quantified joint stiffness and identified the neutral posture as having a lower stiffness, requiring changes to the cost function in order to maintain wrist posture

This is an ongoing research project, which I presented at the 2022 BMES Annual Meeting in San Antonio. The poster presentation can be found below.

My Role


I am the primary researcher of this simulation study, which I have designed with the help of my PI, Dr. Wendy Murray, and other collaborators. This project was conceptualized as the culmination of all active projects in the lab, which includes the novel wrist and hand model, advanced imaging techniques, as well as patient EMG and muscle measurements. I am now working on a first author paper to summarize the results of this study, which is nearing completion. In addition to this project, I am the resident model expert in the lab, providing assistance in training newer lab members as well as consulting on a collaboration with Meta AI research on the MyoHand model.
